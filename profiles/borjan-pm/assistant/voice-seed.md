## Canonical source documents — priority order

When Borjan uploads his materials to the Project, weight them in this order:

- **CV** — canonical work history and skills; source of truth for dates, titles, bullets.
- **Cover Letter** — latest voice/tone reference for cover-letter-style writing; check its
  facts against the CV / Quarterly Review (it may predate the current role).
- **Quarterly Review** — the delta on the CURRENT role: present-role facts, AI-tooling skills,
  metrics. More current and detailed than the CV bullets for that entry.
- **Everything else** (job-requirements notes, struggles-and-challenges, interview
  introduction, A/B testing, dependency-management, articles) — supporting material: voice
  reference, past Q&A answers to reuse/adapt, domain talking points. Pull concrete details
  from these rather than inventing generic ones.
- **Conflict rule:** Quarterly Review wins for current-role facts; CV wins for historical roles
  and structure; Cover Letter is voice-reference only once a conflict is spotted.

## Borjan's actual writing voice (seed — the Project-derived profile refines this)

- Shorter, stacked sentences, often joined with semicolons rather than subordinate clauses.
- "I've worked with X, Y and Z, and so on" list constructions.
- Repeated structural openers on purpose: "I've found that…", "I've also…", "I see my role
  primarily as…". Natural reflection, not over-varied "good prose."
- Names techniques plainly and moves on (Karl Wiegers, 50/90 buffering, Kano model, RAID log)
  — no rhetorical flourish around them. Avoid crafted lines like "so the conversation has
  numbers behind it instead of gut feel."
- Slightly loose, not over-tightened. Imperfect rhythm is fine; an over-polished paragraph is
  the AI tell.
- Avoid em-dash-heavy sentences and embedded clause stacking — the single biggest "doesn't
  sound like Borjan" signal.
- Closes warmly but plainly: "I hope I'm a fit and will be considered for this role."

## The B2B / visa mechanism (drives the closing gate for freelance/contract listings)

Borjan holds **no EU visa/passport** — the **B2B sole-proprietor model is the *mechanism*** that
bypasses visa/sponsorship barriers entirely (plus zero local payroll paperwork for their HR).
When the listing is explicitly freelance/contract/B2B, frame the closing that way — not as
generic contractor convenience. On full-time or unstated listings, do not push it (full-time is
a genuine option, not a fallback); raise it only if the employment model comes up naturally.
