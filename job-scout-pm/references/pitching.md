- **CV (Borjan_Petreski_CV_June2026.docx)** — canonical work history and skills. Source of truth for dates, titles, bullets.
- **Cover Letter (Cover_Letter.pdf)** — latest voice/tone reference for cover-letter-style writing; check its facts against CV/Quarterly Review (it may predate the current role).
- **Quarterly Review (Borjan_Petreski_Quarterly_Review_2026Q2.pdf)** — the delta on the CURRENT role (2Coders Studio / BritBox Apollo): present-role facts, AI tooling skills list, metrics. More current and detailed than the CV bullets for that entry.
- **Everything else** (Job Requirements, Struggles and Challenges, Interview Introduction, AB Testing, Dependency Management, articles) — supporting material: voice reference, past Q&A answers to reuse/adapt, domain talking points. Pull concrete details from these rather than inventing generic ones.
- **Conflict rule:** Quarterly Review wins for current-role facts; CV wins for historical roles and structure; Cover Letter is voice-reference only once a conflict is spotted.

**AI tooling terminology — always Gemini, never "the ChatGPT API":** older source docs (Cover_Letter, Interview_Introduction, AB_Testing) reference ChatGPT — legacy; correct to **Gemini** on output, silently, no flagging.

**No invented experience:** flag genuine gaps directly rather than papering over them. "Insufficient data" beats a guess.

## Direct email pitches (vs portal cover letters)

Two-step task when Borjan provides a JD and asks for a hiring-manager email pitch:
1. Identify the core business problem the company/role is solving — a sentence or two of chat commentary, no fluff.
2. Write the pitch in his voice (below), peer-to-peer direct email. No generic openers ("I am thrilled to apply..."), no corporate fluff, no AI tropes ("delve," "testament," "moreover," em-dash-heavy sentences).

---

## Borjan's actual writing voice

- Shorter, stacked sentences, often joined with semicolons rather than subordinate clauses.
- "I've worked with X, Y and Z, and so on" list constructions.
- Repeated structural openers on purpose: "I've found that...", "I've also...", "I see my role primarily as...". Natural reflection, not over-varied "good prose."
- Names techniques plainly and moves on (Karl Wiegers, 50/90 buffering, Kano model, RAID log) — no rhetorical flourish around them. Avoid crafted lines like "so the conversation has numbers behind it instead of gut feel."
- Slightly loose, not over-tightened. Imperfect rhythm is fine; an over-polished paragraph is the AI tell.
- Avoid em-dash-heavy sentences and embedded clause stacking — the single biggest "doesn't sound like Borjan" signal.
- Closes warmly but plainly: "I hope I'm a fit and will be considered for this role."

**First-draft discipline:** apply this voice on the FIRST draft, not as a correction after Borjan flags it. Re-read the relevant source doc (Cover_Letter, Interview_Introduction, or an article) before drafting if it hasn't been referenced recently in the conversation.

## Closing paragraph — gate on the listing's actual employment type

- **Explicitly freelance/contract/B2B listing:** close by naturally working in the B2B contractor advantage (immediate integration, no visa/payroll overhead for their HR) as the final paragraph — not bolted on.
- **Full-time (or ambiguous/unstated):** do NOT push the B2B framing. Close with a plain, direct availability/interest statement — full-time is a genuine option, never a fallback. Raise B2B/contractor only if the employment model comes up naturally later (e.g. interview question).
- **Genuinely unclear which model applies:** ask Borjan once rather than guessing — this is the closing choice most likely to misfire if assumed.

**Why the B2B angle matters when it applies:** Borjan holds no EU visa/passport — the B2B sole-proprietor model is the *mechanism* that bypasses visa/sponsorship barriers entirely (plus zero local payroll paperwork for their HR). Frame it that way, not as generic contractor convenience.

## Delivery format — always copy-blocks

Any submittable application text (cover letters, email pitches, form answers, follow-up screening answers) goes inside a plain markdown code block (triple backticks, no language tag) — one-tap copyable on mobile, delivered on the first response without a second request. Commentary and analysis stay as normal chat prose outside the block.

## Job hunt strategy & tone constraints (canonical)

- Regional focus: "Worldwide," "EMEA," or explicitly open to non-EU applicants.
- Tone: professional, confident, direct peer-to-peer. Never: "I am thrilled to apply," emojis, "delve"/"testament"/"moreover," generic corporate fluff.

## Common application-form question types (beyond the cover letter)

Many employers (Human Made confirmed) follow the cover letter with a Q&A form. Draft in the same voice, not a separate "interview answer" register.

- **Portfolio / live project examples** → honestly: most work sits behind client NDAs; name what's public (e.g. The Culture Trip) and offer a walkthrough on a call.
- **Specialisms / tools / experience levels** → structured list, with years and a level (expert/advanced/intermediate) per item, not prose.
- **Dislikes / weaker areas** → honest, constructive, grounded in real past examples (Scrumfall, dispersed stakeholders, time-tracking imposed on engineers, authority-building with senior teams).
- **Preferred engagement length** → upfront that most experience is long-form engagements, but note the discrete shorter-scope deliverables within them (audits, A&D phases); state flexibility toward the platform's actual model.
